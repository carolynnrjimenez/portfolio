Reading & Professional Growth

As 2025 wrapped up, I reflected on the books that shaped my thinking as an educator, collaborator, and human. Each book helped me grow in my teaching, leadership, and understanding of the world.

Onward & The PD Book — Elena Aguilar

I started Onward in November 2024 and carried “Resilience” as a personal mantra throughout the year, returning to the monthly exercises as grounding anchors during a heavy and uncertain time. As I facilitated learning with adults, Aguilar’s work helped me rethink what it means to support growth beyond the classroom. The PD Book arrived as a recommnedation from a colleague at exactly the right moment!

Building Thinking Classrooms — Peter Liljedahl

A powerful push to reimagine what rigor looks like when students are centered as thinkers, not answer-getters.

Learning How to Learn — Barbara Oakley

A young adult–friendly exploration of how learning works, with tools I wished more students had access to earlier in their educational journeys.

Atlas of AI — Kate Crawford

Essential reading for anyone working with technology who wants to understand the hidden labor, environmental costs, and power structures behind “innovation.”

Just Mercy — Bryan Stevenson

A bridge between subjects, helping students connect what they were reading in English to modeling and transformations of functions in math — and a reminder of the power of integrated learning.

Dragon Hoops — Gene Luen Yang

Storytelling and joy used to examine educator identity and the complicated feelings around leaving the classroom, rooted in the Bay Area.

Mindful of Race — Ruth King

Key mindfulness tools to better understand, sit with, and engage constructively in moments of racial tension, both personally and in community.

Getting Things Done — David Allen

Practical, grounding, and surprisingly helpful for creating mental space so meaningful work can actually happen.

Taken together, these books reflect a year shaped by learning alongside others, stretching into new roles, and thinking deeply about how we teach, learn, and care for one another. I’m always grateful for the people who recommend the right book at the right time.
